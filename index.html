STRABooth: Spatial-Temporal Representation Alignment for Customized Video Generation

Under review on Siggraph Asia 2025

ANONYMOUS AUTHOR(S)

Customized video generation aims to produce videos that faithfully preserve the subject’s appearance from reference images while maintaining temporally consistent motion from reference videos. Existing methods focus on preserving local information for spatial and motion features but lack the capability to effectively capture and control global subject identity and coherent motion patterns. To address this limitation, we propose STRABooth, a novel framework based on global representation alignment in spatial and temporal dimension respectively, as well as stage-by-stage subject and motion customization. To achieve global spatial control in subject learning, we leverage global representations from reference images via a self-supervised vision encoder to guide spatial representation alignment, enabling the model to capture the subject's global structure and improve high-level semantic consistency beyond local details. For motion learning, we utilize global temporal representations of optical flow from reference videos to capture structurally coherent, object-level motion trajectories decoupled from appearance. These global supervisory signals provide holistic guidance over both appearance and motion during training, significantly enhancing subject fidelity and motion controllability. To better leverage the global spatial and temporal representation information learned in the first two customization stages, we introduce a spatial-temporal association injection strategy, which aligns with the coarse-to-fine nature of diffusion generation, emphasizing motion guidance in the early steps and appearance refinement in the later ones. Extensive experiments demonstrate that STRABooth achieves state-of-the-art performance in subject and motion customization, maintaining consistency in subject details and motion patterns, demonstrating its effectiveness for controllable text-to-video generation.
